Benefits of Going Solar

Decisions we make as consumers have an impact on our lives and on the world around us. Going solar can affect the climate, the economy, and our own pocketbooks.


Here are some important benefits to home solar:

1. Lower Electricity Bills

The most common yet crucial benefit of going solar for any homeowner, business or non-profits is that you can dramatically reduce your electricity costs. Since electricity prices are unpredictable and will mostly likely continue to rise, that’ll make managing your budget difficult. By installing solar panel system, you also fix your electricity costs and make them more predictable with lifespan of nearly 25 years. This is especially beneficial for anyone with a fixed income, as well as for businesses who stand to gain from a more stable cash flow. When you make your electricity costs more predictable by going solar, you also improve your expense forecasting and management capabilities.

2. Net Energy Metering (NEM) can work for you.

Net Energy Metering allows customers who generate their own electricity from solar panels to sell the excess electricity they aren't using back into the grid. Simply putting, If energy generation < energy consumed: owner pays just for the net amount. If energy generation > energy consumed: the owner gets credit for excess generation. However, NEM policies differs from state to state and solar company like ‘NxtGen Power’ will be able to explain more about NEM as per customer’s location.

3. MNRE and Tax Benefits

Ministry of India offers a 40% subsidy on capital costs for units that use up to 3KW solar power and 20% subsidy for larger units that use up to 10KW for residential consumers. 40% accelerated tax depreciation for commercial and industrial consumers. NxtGen handles all licensing(paper work) on behalf of customer.

4. Increases the value of your Property

Multiple studies have found that homes equipped with solar energy systems have higher property values and sell more quickly than non-solar homes. Appraisers are increasingly taking solar installations into consideration as they value homes at the time of a sale, and as homebuyers become more educated about solar, demand for properties equipped with solar panel systems will continue to grow.

5. Energy Independence

The adoption of solar energy is one way to achieve energy independence, reducing your reliance on the public utility grid. The more domestic solar energy we use as a country, the less we will need to rely on foreign imports of carbon-based fossil fuels, contributing to greater economic, political, and environmental stability.

6. Environment Sustainability

We all need clean air for survival. But ground-level ozone and other pollutants from cars, power plants, industrial boilers, refineries, and chemical plants can kill. The damage extends to crops, trees, and other vegetation and the toll on human life is significant. Pollution is so bad in some parts of the country that people are instructed to stay indoors. Utilizing solar power for generating electricity allows us to reduce the harmful emission of greenhouse gases and other types of pollutants. A typical 10kw system benefits the environment by reducing 308 tonnes of carbon emission which is equivalent to planting 490 teak trees for a lifetime.

7. Sun light is abundant

The sun is a renewable resource. Unlike fossil fuels, sunlight is never going away. You can count on it every day. Coal and natural gas are not free, and they are expensive and dirty. Their price is volatile too and has increased systematically over the past decade. But there is no fuel cost for sunlight and will always remain abundantly free.

8. Solar Rooftop integration with EV Charger

With remarkable steps being taken in the EV sector, there is substantial potential within the industry for the growth of the solar industry as well. For the EV sector, perhaps one of the biggest hurdles is keeping the batteries charged, and setting up charging infrastructure to facilitate the same. The good news is that an electric vehicle can be powered by your residential solar system. You can use clean power from the sun to fuel your car, and run your home. The switch from fossil fuels to solar energy contributes to healthy communities and a healthy planet.

9. Earn a great return on your investment

Solar panels aren’t an expense – they’re one of the best ways to invest, with returns rivalling those of more traditional investments like stocks and bonds. Thanks to substantial electricity bill savings, the average Indian homeowner pays off their solar panel system in six to seven years and sees an ROI of 20 percent or more.

10. Create jobs and help your local economy

Solar energy adoption will not only help India’s transition towards a green energy economy but also offer a great solar jobs opportunity that'll trim down poverty in rural areas, besides unleashing a wave of entrepreneurship wherever solar power strikes roots. As per the report from International Renewable Energy Agency(IRENA) the possible boom in rooftop solar in India will eventually led to a million plus jobs and more in the general consensus by 2021
